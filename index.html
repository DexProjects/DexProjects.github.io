Patent Invalidity Searches

    Mitigating risk one character at a time



    DexPatent’s invalidity searches or knock out searches help you to identify prior art – i.e. patent and non-patent documents – that may challenge the validity of the claims of a target patent in order to discover prior art that patent examiner may possibly have overlooked during examination and hence helping you in litigation and licensing related negotiations.


    When is an validity search needed

    The goal of an invalidity search is to locate evidence that the patent claims were granted erroneously due to either oversight or concealment of the prior art during the examination process. Validity searches often are the result of potential patent infringement or a potentially profitable business opportunity.


    Examples & use cases of invalidity search

    1.Invalidate a patent

    A software company has been sued for patent infringement. In response, the company hires DexPatent to perform a search to invalidate the patent of the accuser.

    2.You are considering
     a law suit

    You, the owner of an automotive company and an infringed patent, is considering a lawsuit. In preparation, the company asks DexPatent team to conduct a search to validate its patent as a precursor to enforcement.

    3. Licensing

    You, the patent owner and licensor of a novel technology request DexPatent patent scientists to conduct a validity search, knowing that a highly defensible patent will command greater royalties


    What needs to be searched in a validity search

    An invalidity/validity search involves conducting a patent and non-patent literature search to identify potential references predating the earliest filing of the target patent. The claims of the target patent are to be thoroughly analyzed against identifying closest prior art that may be alone or in combination with another prior art may invalidate the target patent..



    Our deliverables

    Our invalidity/validity search report includes the bibliographic details of potential patent and non-patent literature and the claim comparison matrix containing mapping of the target patent claims to the relevant text of the potential literature.
